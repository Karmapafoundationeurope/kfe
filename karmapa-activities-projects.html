Projects

It is very early for the Karmapa Foundation Europe (KFE) to have full-fledged projects which address all of its main objectives; however up to know two major projects are ongoing:

Construction of the KFE website

The construction and regular update of an effective Karmapa Foundation Europe (KFE) website is very important given:

  • the worldwide awareness of the Karmapa
  • the number of centres which are involved in teaching and practicing Kagyu Buddhism
  • the mission of the KFE as laid down in its statues

The development of the KFE website is a stand-alone project. There are four expected results from its construction:

  • i) support for the first ever visit of His Holiness to Europe
  • ii) support for the Teachings of His Holiness
  • iii) spiritual support for European citizens
  • iv) sponsoring of education, health and other projects

These four results will serve the website’s purpose: “to become a major operational tool for the KFE and establish a regular and effective connection between HH and Europe” which by its turn will contribute to a wider overall objective: “to support all those who have connections with and/or interest in the dharma to benefit all beings”.

The website focus on European citizens, Members of European Kagyu Centres, but also other Tibetan or Buddhist Centres, KFE focal points in Europe, Research centres, organisations, universities and the academia in Europe, but also any other interested person worldwide.

The construction started in the beginning of 2012 in full coordination with the official representative of His Holiness in Europe, Ringu Tulku Rinpoche, the founder of the KFE, but also with the contribution of several volunteers.

Establishment of the KFE office in Brussels

The second project to be undertaken by KFE is the establishment of a Centre in Brussels responsible for the first ever visit of His Holiness the Karmapa in Europe. The centre of operations has already been found and it is now equipped with the kind contribution of the KFE Trustees. It is expected to be fully operational in November 2012. A ceremony of pujas and prayers is expected to take place depending on the availability of Ringu Tulku Rinpoche. The KFE Centre upon its full operation will also serve to provide a secluded learning centre for followers of the Karmapa.
